Số đỏ – Vũ Trọng Phụng

The novel "Red Number" was published in "Hanoi Newspaper" from issue 40 on October 7, 1936 and was first printed as a book in 1938. This famous work has been adapted into many films and plays. This is considered the most famous work in the creative career of the "King of Northern Reportage". "Red Number" revolves around the character who turned Hanoi crazy in the 1930s - 1940s, Red Hair Xuan - from an orphan boy, making a living by doing all kinds of jobs: climbing tamarins, climbing crocodiles, picking up balls at the tennis court, advertising illegal drugs... thanks to cunning tricks, "thanks to timing" he became the director of Xuan newspaper, a social reformer, a tennis professor, even a hero who saved the country, a great man... History Using the contrast between the depravity and immorality with the comedy and satire has helped the novel succeed in exposing the "freaks" of the era in the transition period. Since then, the work has also bitterly criticized the swindling bourgeois society, which is following a pseudo-civilized, extremely ridiculous and corrupt lifestyle. Besides, the work also attacks the movements encouraged by the colonialists such as: Europeanization movement, sports, Buddhist revival... The author's success also lies in building the The characters became typical socio-psychological examples, and to this day, the silhouettes of those characters are still around us somewhere. Author

Vũ Trọng Phụng

Vũ Trọng Phụng is a renowned Vietnamese author, best known for his novel "Số đỏ" (Red Number). He was born in 1912 in Nam Định Province, Vietnam. Phụng's works often depict the social issues and struggles faced by the Vietnamese people during the early 20th century. "Số đỏ" is considered one of his most influential works, exposing the corruption and hypocrisy of the bourgeois society in Vietnam at that time. Phụng's writing style combines satire, comedy, and social commentary, making his works both entertaining and thought-provoking. Despite facing censorship and criticism, Phụng's literary contributions have left a lasting impact on Vietnamese literature.
