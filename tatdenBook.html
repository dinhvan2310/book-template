Tắt đèn – Ngô Tất Tố

Turn Off the Lights by author Ngo Tat To is one of the most unique literary works of Vietnamese literature. Not only is it a story about the difficult lives of Vietnamese farmers in the early twentieth century, Turn Off the Lights also honestly and deeply reflects the impact of the French empire on the lives of the Vietnamese people.

In Turn Off the Lights, we are introduced to the main character, Ms. Dau and her family, a typical image of impoverishment and misery in a state of heavy tax collection. The work vividly shows the life of this family, with emotional and touching details. With every word and every literary detail, Ngo Tat To made readers feel the bitterness and pain that Ms. Dau's family had to go through. Thereby, Turn Off the Lights contributes to arousing compassion and finding good feelings in readers, especially teenagers.

With profound content and genuine emotions, Turn Off the Lights is considered an "immortal" literary work of Vietnamese literature. Thus, this is an indispensable book for those who love Vietnamese literature, especially those who want to understand more deeply about the country's history and culture.
