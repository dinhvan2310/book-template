Author

Nguyễn Tuân

Nguyen Tuan (July 10, 1910 - July 28, 1987) was a Vietnamese writer, with a knack for essays and memoirs. Born and raised in Hang Bac Street, Hanoi; His hometown is Thuong Dinh village, Nhan Muc commune (literally Moc village), now belongs to Nhan Chinh ward, Thanh Xuan district, Hanoi. Nguyen Tuan grew up in a Confucian family when Chinese learning was in decline. His father was Nguyen An Lan - a talented and patriotic scholar who lived under the feudal colonial regime. From a young age, he was greatly influenced by his father. During his youth, family life was very difficult so he had to migrate to many places and Thanh Hoa was the place he lived the longest. Nguyen Tuan is a famous writer, considered a master in composing and using the Vietnamese language. His writing style is unique and rich in language. In the process of writing literature, Nguyen Tuan also used a number of pen names such as Nhat Lang, Thanh Thuy, Ngoc Loi Quat, An Ngu Tuyen, Tuan Thua Sac, Thanh Ha, Ngoc Loi Nhat. In 1996, Nguyen Tuan was posthumously awarded the Ho Chi Minh Prize for literature and arts. To commemorate the "pen master" in Hanoi and many other provinces and cities across the country, roads and streets were named after him.
