Truyện Kiều – Nguyễn Du

In our nation's thousands of years of history, there has never been a work as beloved by the people as The Tale of Kieu. Although national political institutions change over time, people's passion for The Tale of Kieu has not changed. Why? Dao Nguyen Pho, in the preface to the book Doan Truong Tan Thanh (also known as The Tale of Kieu) published in 1902, replied: "The words are beautiful but flowery, the tone is resonant but round, the materials chosen are very broad, the events It is told very well, gleaning the romantic love songs of the ancients, and also contributing to the country's proverbs, salty, trivial, rustic and elegant... making people laugh, making people cry, making people cry. Some people are happy, it makes others sad, it makes people turn it over and over again a thousand times, the more they recite it, the more they never get bored. It's truly a wonderful Southern song, such a popular love tune."

Author

Nguyễn Du

Nguyen Du is the nation's great poet, the most famous poet of Vietnam, a diplomat, and a great mandarin under the Nguyen Dynasty. Nguyen Du's name and works have been known to posterity for a long time, but few people remember that he died of an epidemic (cholera), during a pandemic in which more than 200,000 people died nationwide, according to experts. unofficial statistics (1820). He passed away when his talent was ripe, leaving behind endless grief. In 1965, the 200th anniversary of Nguyen Du's birth (he was born on January 3, 1766) was solemnly held, both in the North and the South, causing widespread international resonance. "The Tale of Kieu" has been translated into more than 30 languages, many domestic and foreign writers and artists have vividly adapted this work into many different art forms, making an important contribution to introducing the work. to the domestic and international public, and participate in preserving, promoting and promoting the nation's cultural heritage.
